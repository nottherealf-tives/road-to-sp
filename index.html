The Plan

A phased roadmap from now to the 2027 internship cycle.

How the phases work

Ten courses at once is not realistic — they are sequenced, not stacked. Each phase swaps in new courses while gym and the Optiver mental-math drill stay daily.

Phase 1 · Foundations (now–Aug): CS50 Python, both Bloomberg courses, and Harvard STAT 110 — the keystone, so it runs daily.

Phase 2 · Stats & Fin-Eng (Sep–Dec): Princeton ORF 245, Columbia IEOR E4706, MIT 15.450.

Phase 3 · Advanced + Interviews (Jan–May 2027): Stanford MS&E 246, CMU 46-923, NYU Courant, plus an interview-prep ramp.

Honest priority note

Jane Street interviews lean on probability, mental math, brain-teasers and clean coding far more than finance theory. STAT 110, the Optiver drill, and the monthly puzzles are your highest-leverage work — protect those blocks first. The finance-engineering courses are great background but secondary for the interview itself.

2027 summer-internship applications open early — roughly mid-to-late 2026 — so treat Phase 1 as your real runway.
